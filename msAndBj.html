Informing the Community

The duty to inform communities adjacent to volcanoes about the dangers that will ensue following an eruption. Journalists work closely with scientists to determine when a volcanic eruption will take place, and it is the journalists job to inform the community as soon as possible. If reports are accurate and released prior to an eruption it will allow citizens in the danger zone to evacuate with their lives.

Indonesian Volcano Eruption

Recently, Mount Soputan in northern Sulawesi, Indonesia erupted causing a mass number of casualties and damages to the local communities. It is the journalist's duty to report the damages inflicted by the eruption. By reporting the information, people can donate money from across the globe in order to help rescue efforts.

Journalists on Scene

Regardless of the natural disaster, journalists must have no fear and travel to the destination. Field reports gather information directly from victims and the surrounding area. Informing people around the globe is vital for the recovery of the destination inflicted by a natural disaster.
